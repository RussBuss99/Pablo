THE STORY OF PABLO

✓ GOOD BOY ✓ DOG FRIENDLY ✓ LOVES PEOPLE ✓ CURIOUS ABOUT CATS ✓ BUILT FOR MOVEMENT ✓ READY FOR HOME

Pablo's not the type to dwell on the past. But like any real album, there are some heavy tracks before the light breaks through.

He came up under a young owner who wasn't ready—long days, longer absences, sometimes gone for days straight. No structure, no consistency. And for a minute, it looked like Pablo's whole story might get scrapped before it had a chance to breathe. Before it had a chance to become what it was always supposed to be.

Then somebody who knew better stepped in.

An older man in the building saw Pablo—really saw him—and pulled him out of that situation. No fanfare. Just a quiet act of love from someone who recognized a good soul when he met one. For a while, it was everything Pablo needed. But life don't pause for anybody. His rescuer's health started slipping, and Pablo—big, wired, built for movement—needs more than the current situation can hold. The walks got hard. The pulls got stronger. Everybody knows it's time.

So here we are. New track loading.

Pablo loves like he's been saving it up. You walk through the door and he acts like you just ended a world tour and came home specifically for him. He runs with other dogs like they're his crew, leans into you like he's known you forever, and carries this big ridiculous personality that fills whatever room he's in. He's not subtle about it. He doesn't know how to be.

He's curious about cats—respectful, not chaotic—though they might need a second to adjust to the frequency he's operating on.

What Pablo needs is somebody present. Somebody active. Somebody who isn't going to treat him like an afterthought between other things. He's been patient. He's been through it. And he's still—somehow—one of the most loving animals you'll ever meet.

He doesn't need a perfect home. He just needs his home.

This isn't Pablo's outro.
This is the album finally finding its audience.
